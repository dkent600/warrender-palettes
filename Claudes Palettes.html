Claude's Carolyn Warrender Color Palettes

Based on Johannes Itten's Color Star System

Carolyn Warrender's "Living with Color" system uses Johannes Itten's color theory with 12 colors in each palette: 3 primary, 3 secondary (made by mixing two primaries), and 6 tertiary colors (made by mixing two secondaries). Each of the six elemental palettes plus Naturals is divided into warm (yellow/orange undertones) and cool (blue undertones) variations, giving you 12 harmonious colors to work with in each temperature.

About the Itten Color System: Johannes Itten's color theory forms the foundation of Carolyn Warrender's palettes. Each palette contains 3 primary colors (red, yellow, blue), 3 secondary colors (created by mixing two primaries: orange from red+yellow, green from yellow+blue, purple from red+blue), and 6 tertiary colors (created by mixing adjacent secondaries). The warm and cool variations shift the undertones while maintaining this 12-color structure, creating harmonious color schemes for every decorating style.
